# LNTJOQXBM 115-in-1 Precision Screwdriver Set — 115 bits for but you get what you pay for If you're repairing phones or laptops regularly, you already know that a stripped screw can ruin your day. The LNTJOQXBM 115-in-1 set promises a bit for every job at a price that's hard to ignore. I've been through enough cheap tool kits to know the difference between "115 pieces" and "115 useful pieces" — so let's sort that out. The specs show that listing claims this is a professional-grade electronics repair kit with magnetic bits, an ergonomic handle, and a carrying case. At this price point, "professional" usually means "works for the occasional repair, don't bet your livelihood on it." The specs confirm this: metal bits, plastic case, and a 3.6 out of 5 star rating from 15 reviews. That's not poor, but it's not confidence-inspiring either. ## What you're actually getting The bit selection is the headline here. You get Phillips, flathead, Torx, hex, and specialty bits — 115 total. For a precision screwdriver set in this price range, that's a lot of variety. You'll find bits for iPhone pentalobe screws, laptop hinges, game console controllers, watch backs, and eyeglass hinges. The magnetic handle and bits are supposed to keep space-saving screws from disappearing into your carpet, which is the main thing you want from a precision set. The purple-handled screwdriver has an ergonomic grip, and the carrying case keeps everything organized. Looking at the data, case material is listed as metal for the bits and plastic for the case itself — which is standard at this price. The handle is metal, which is better than the all-plastic handles you'll find on some competing kits at the same price. The magnetic claim is worth noting: strong magnetic handle and bits. For precision work, you want enough magnetism to hold a screw but not so much that it magnetizes the screwdriver shaft and interferes with sensitive electronics. The listing doesn't specify the magnetic strength, so you'll need to test this yourself. Here is another way to look at it. ## Where it falls short The biggest red flag is the 3.6 out of 5 star rating from only 15 reviews. That's a space-saving sample size, and the rating is mediocre. For context, most precision screwdriver sets in this category average 4.0 to 4.5 stars after 100+ reviews. The low rating and few reviews suggest either a latest product or one that hasn't impressed buyers. What the specifications tell us is that the listing doesn't specify the bit hardness (usually measured in HRC for steel bits), the exact magnetic strength, or the handle material beyond "metal." For a set claiming to be "professional quality," the absence of these details is telling. Most reputable precision bit sets at this price point will at least mention the steel grade or hardness. There's also no mention of warranty, which is common for ultra-budget tools. If a bit snaps on the first use — and at this price, that's a real possibility — you're buying a replacement. ## Who this is for This set makes sense if you're a hobbyist who occasionally repairs phones, laptops, or game consoles and wants many bits without spending much. If you're doing one or two repairs a month and don't want to hunt for individual bits, the 115-piece count gives you coverage for most common electronics screws. Based on the available information, magnetic handle is genuinely useful for compact screws, and the carrying case keeps things organized. ## Who should look elsewhere If you're a professional technician doing repairs daily, skip this. The low review count and mediocre rating suggest inconsistent quality. You're better off spending more on a set from iFixit, Wiha, or Xiaomi's precision kit — those have documented steel hardness, reliable magnetism, and warranty support. Also avoid this if you need to work on tight spaces where a bulky handle would get in the way — the purple handle looks comfortable but isn't slim. Who this is for: Hobbyists and occasional repairers who want a broad bit selection for under and don't mind taking a chance on quality. Decent for the toolbox you grab when a friend's phone requires a new battery. Who should look elsewhere: Daily-use technicians, anyone working on investment-grade equipment where a stripped screw could damage the device, and buyers who prefer known brands with warranty support.
