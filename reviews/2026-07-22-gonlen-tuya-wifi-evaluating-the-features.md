Title: GONLEN Tuya WiFi Temperature Sensor — Remote monitoring with a long probe, but limited by 2.4GHz-only WiFi and a short backup battery If you need to keep tabs on a freezer, greenhouse, or pool temperature from your phone, a WiFi-enabled sensor with an external probe is the practical answer. The GONLEN Tuya WiFi Temperature Sensor covers that use case with a 9.8-foot cable and a waterproof probe, but a few spec-level quirks are worth noting before you buy. The core setup is straightforward: a main panel sits indoors (it isn't waterproof), connected to a probe on a 3-meter cable that you drop into a freezer, water tank, or greenhouse soil. The specs show that probe itself is waterproof and rated for -40°C to +120°C, which covers everything from deep freeze to hot water applications. The main panel has a digital display showing real-time temperature, and the Tuya Smart or Smart Life app mirrors that reading remotely via WiFi or 4G.

 That 9.8-foot cable is the standout feature here. Most WiFi temperature sensors in this class ship with a probe cable half that length, so you have genuine flexibility to reach into a chest freezer or across a greenhouse bench. The probe itself is thin (5mm diameter, 45mm long), so it fits through space-saving gaskets or seals without breaking them. The alarm system works as described: you set upper and lower limits in the app, and when the temperature crosses either threshold, the panel beeps for about 30 seconds and pushes a notification to your phone. Looking at the data, buzzer is fixed at 30 seconds with no way to adjust or silence it early — something to know if you are placing the panel in a bedroom or quiet space. The built-in 600mAh battery is a backup, not a primary power source. The unit needs to be plugged into a DC5V adapter (included) for normal operation. When the power goes out, the battery keeps the sensor running for about 4 to 5 hours, which is enough for a short outage but not for extended monitoring. The spec sheet does not say how long it takes to recharge the battery, so plan for a full day plugged in after use. here's another way to look at it. the biggest limitation is WiFi compatibility. What the specifications tell us is that the unit only works on 2.4GHz networks — no 5GHz support. If your router broadcasts both bands under one SSID (most modern ones do), you will need to temporarily disable the 5GHz band or use a separate 2.4GHz guest network during setup. that's a common annoyance with budget IoT devices, but it's worth flagging if you have a mesh system that doesn't easily separate bands. Data logging is fundamental: the system records about one data point per hour and stores seven days of history. You can export that as a file from the app. For casual monitoring — checking if your freezer stayed, we found that cold overnight — that is fine. If you need granular minute-by-minute tracking for a lab or fermentation project, this will not cut it. The temperature can be calibrated through the app, which is a nice touch. Factory default is Fahrenheit, but you can switch to Celsius in the app settings. Based on the available information, panel itself has a manual button for unit switching as well. Summary
This sensor suits anyone who needs to monitor a single location remotely, especially where a long probe cable is required — think freezer, pool, or greenhouse. The ±1°C accuracy is standard for this price tier, and the alarm system is functional. However, the 2.4GHz-only WiFi, short backup battery, and hourly logging rate limit its usefulness for power users or anyone needing precise, frequent data. Who this is for
This makes sense if you have a dedicated 2.4GHz WiFi network and need to keep an eye on a single freezer, water tank, or greenhouse from your phone. The long probe cable is the real selling point — it saves you from having to place the panel near the monitored area. Someone who wants a simple "did it stay within range?" check with push alerts will find this adequate. Who should look elsewhere
Skip this if you need minute-by-minute temperature logging, or if your home network is 5GHz-only and you cannot easily create a 2.4GHz segment. The 4-5 hour backup battery also means this is not a solution for long power outages. ### Frequently Asked Questions About the GONLEN Tuya WiFi
### Can I put the probe in water or outdoors?
Yes, the probe and cable are waterproof, so you can submerge them in water or use them outdoors. However, the main panel is not waterproof and must be kept indoors.

### Does this sensor work with 5G Wi-Fi?
No, it only supports 2.4G Wi-Fi. If your router combines both bands, you'll need to switch to the 2.4G network during setup.

### How does the temperature alarm work?
You set an upper and lower temperature limit in the app. If the temperature goes outside that range, the device buzzer sounds for about 30 seconds and the app sends a push notification.

### Can I see temperature history?
Yes, the system records temperature about once per hour and stores up to 7 days of data. You can view and export that history from the app.

### Does it need to be plugged in all the time?
Yes, it requires a constant DC5V power supply via the included USB adapter. It has a built-in rechargeable battery that can keep it running for about 4–5 hours during a power outage.
