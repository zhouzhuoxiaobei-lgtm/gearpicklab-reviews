HHSOET Metal Wall Mount Power Strip — 6 outlets with 1200J surge protection in a mountable aluminum shell If you're setting up a workbench, workshop, or garage and need power where there's no convenient wall outlet, the HHSOET Metal Wall Mount Power Strip is built for exactly that scenario. It gives you six AC outlets in a slim aluminum housing that screws directly onto a wall, bench, or stud, with a 6-foot cord to reach the nearest outlet. The core specs tell a straightforward story. Here's what the data sheet says:

 What matters here: the 14AWG cord is thicker than the standard 16AWG found on many basic power strips — that's a meaningful difference if you're running multiple tools or devices that draw sustained current. The 1200J surge protection is entry-level; it'll handle minor spikes from nearby appliances but won't save you from a direct lightning hit. For a workshop or garage environment, that's adequate protection for power tools and chargers, but not for sensitive electronics like a computer or audio gear. The specs show that aluminum housing is the standout aspect. Most power strips in this range use all-plastic construction. The metal shell here resists dents, rust, and won't melt or deform under moderate heat. Combined with the flame-retardant PC internal components, this unit is better suited than a plastic strip for environments where sparks, dust, or accidental impacts are possible. The four included screws let you mount it securely — no flimsy adhesive strips or magnets. Limitations & data gaps The spec sheet doesn't mention the weight, which is unusual for a mountable product. Based on the aluminum housing and 14AWG cord, expect it to be heavier than a typical plastic strip — probably around 1-2 pounds. That's fine for permanent mounting but less perfect if you plan to move it around frequently. There's also no information about the switch's rated cycles or the surge protector's response time. Looking at the data, 1200J rating is modest; many competitors in this category offer 2000J or higher. If you're protecting expensive electronics, this isn't the right unit. The outlet spacing is 1.57 inches center-to-center, which is generous enough for most wall warts and bulky plugs, but the 0.6-inch gap between outlets means some oversized adapters will still block adjacent sockets. What the specifications tell us is that the aluminum housing is also a potential conductor if not properly grounded — the spec says it's certified to UL 1363, so it should be, but the listing doesn't clarify grounding details. Summary The HHSOET Metal Wall Mount Power Strip is a solid option for workshops, garages, and workbenches where you need permanent, mountable power with basic surge protection. Its aluminum housing and 14AWG cord are better than what most plastic strips offer at this spec level. Based on the available information, 1200J surge protection is entry-level and the lack of USB ports or a longer cord limits its versatility. It's a practical, no-frills solution for powering tools and shop equipment. Who this is for This power strip makes sense if you're outfitting a workshop, garage, or enclosed bench area where you need six outlets mounted out of the way. Here is what stands out from the specs: aluminum shell and included screws mean you can bolt it to a wall stud or under a workbench and forget about it. The 14AWG cord handles tools like a circular saw or shop vac that draw 10-15 amps without voltage drop issues. It's also a decent choice for a home office desk if you want the outlets off the floor and out from underfoot. Who should look elsewhere If you need surge protection for a home theater, computer assembly, or any sensitive electronics, the 1200J rating is too low — look for 2000J or higher with a higher clamping voltage rating. If you need portability, this unit is designed to be mounted and left in place; the weight and lack of a carrying handle make it awkward to move around. Anyone who wants USB charging ports, a longer cord, or more than six outlets should also skip this and consider a larger model from the same brand's lineup.
