### What to Look For in Kitchen Appliances
 In this analysis, we take a closer look at the MegaWise 5. Kitchen appliances are one of those categories where the difference between a decent buy and a regret is often just a few smart choices. After testing dozens of units since 2018, here’s what I’ve learned to prioritize.

**Power and efficiency.** Don’t get distracted by wattage numbers alone. A 1500W* (based on available data) cooktop that heats unevenly is worse than a 1200W* (based on available data) one with precise temperature control. Look for consistent heat distribution, not just peak output.

**Build quality that matches use.** If you cook daily, plastic hinges and thin metal will fail within a year. For occasional use, lighter materials are fine. Check for stainless steel components on high-use parts like blades, heating elements, and drip trays.

**Ease of cleaning.** This is the single biggest predictor of whether you’ll actually use an appliance. Removable parts that go in the dishwasher beat hand-wash-only designs every time. Non-stick coatings are nice but scratch easily—look for ceramic or hard-anodized surfaces.

**Size and storage.** Measure your counter space before buying. Many “compact” models still take up a surprising amount of room. If you have limited storage, prioritize vertical designs or ones that stack. Products vary quite a bit in terms of cooking performance.

**Noise level.** Manufacturers rarely list decibel ratings, but you can guess from motor type. Induction cooktops are silent. Blenders and mixers are loud. For things like milk frothers or air purifiers, quiet operation matters a lot.

 ### What to Avoid

- **Overhyped “smart” features.** Bluetooth connectivity and app control sound cool but rarely improve cooking. You don’t need your toaster to send notifications.

- **Non-stick coatings that claim to be “scratch-proof.”** They’re not. Ceramic is better but still fragile. Hard-anodized aluminum is the most tough option for cookware.

- **“Space-saving” designs that actually waste space.** Some foldable appliances take up more room when stored than their fixed counterparts. Measure twice.

- **Bundled accessories you won’t use.** A blender that comes with three extra cups and a travel lid might seem like a deal, but you’ll likely use one cup and lose the others.
