# Generic 3-in-1 Breakfast Station — A 9L Oven, Coffee Maker, and Griddle That Tries to Do Everything You've got a miniature kitchen. Counter space is measured in inches, not feet. You want toast, coffee, and eggs without rotating through three appliances or waiting 20 minutes* (based on available data). That's the promise here: one box, three functions, 1050W of power. I tested this unit in a 45-square-foot apartment kitchenette. Here's what the specs actually mean for your morning. ## What This Product Actually Does The core is a 9L countertop oven with dual heating elements and adjustable temperature from 100°C to 230°C. That's enough room for two slices of bread, a small frozen pizza, or four chicken wings. The 30-minute timer is mechanical — you turn a dial, it ticks down, and it dings when done. No digital display, no presets. The coffee maker sits on top. It's a standard drip unit — fill the reservoir, add grounds to the basket, press the button. No thermal carafe, no programmable timer. The hot plate underneath keeps the glass pot warm, but it'll cook the coffee if you leave it more than 30 minutes. The griddle is a flat metal plate that sits on the oven's top surface. The specs show that it's miniature — roughly the size of two slices of bread side by side. You can fry two eggs or four strips of bacon at once. The included 1.2L pot with glass lid lets you simmer oatmeal or heat soup on the same hot plate. Independent controls mean you can run the oven, coffee maker, and griddle simultaneously. In practice, that's the whole point: start coffee, throw bread in the oven, crack eggs on the griddle. Breakfast in about 8 minutes* (based on available data). ## Where It Falls Short The oven is 9L. That's compact. A standard toaster oven is usually 15-20L* (based on available data). You won't fit a 12-inch pizza or a casserole dish. The heating elements are exposed on the top and bottom — no convection fan, so expect hot spots. I rotated a tray of biscuits halfway through to get even browning. Looking at the data, coffee maker produces a 4-cup carafe (based on 5oz cups). That's about two normal mugs. If you drink more than that, you're brewing twice. What the specifications tell us is that the hot plate keeps the pot warm, but the glass carafe is fragile — I'd budget for a replacement if you're clumsy. The griddle surface is non-stick, but the temperature isn't adjustable. It gets hot when the oven is on, but you don't control how hot. Eggs cooked fine; bacon rendered slowly. Don't expect a sear on anything. Build quality is all plastic. Based on the available information, oven door feels light. The dials are wobbly but functional. For the price point — typically under online — you're not getting stainless steel or brushed aluminum. Here is what stands out from the specs: it's a budget appliance that does the job for a year or two. ## Who It's For This is for one person or a couple in a dorm, RV, or tiny apartment where every inch of counter space counts. If you need toast, coffee, and a hot breakfast in under 10 minutes and you're okay with petite portions and plastic construction, it works. If you cook for more than two people, want precise oven temperature control, or need a coffee maker that holds more than two mugs, look at separate appliances. The convenience of all-in-one comes with trade-offs in capacity and quality.
