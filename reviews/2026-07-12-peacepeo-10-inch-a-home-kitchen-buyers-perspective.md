Title: PeacePeo 10 Inch Round & Oval Sourdough Proofing Basket Set — A complete starter kit with real utility, but check the scale’s limits If you’re getting into sourdough and don’t want to piece together a dozen separate purchases, this set from PeacePeo throws in just about everything you might need. Two banneton baskets (round and oval), a starter jar, a kitchen scale, a bread lame, a Danish whisk, a silicone sling, a baking mat, a cutting board, a bread knife, and even stencil molds. That’s 20-something items in one box. For someone starting from zero, that’s the main appeal. The two 10-inch rattan baskets are the core of the kit. They’re natural, chemical-free rattan, which is standard for this category—it wicks moisture from the dough surface during proofing, helping develop a firmer crust. The specs show that liners are included and removable, which makes cleanup easier. The round basket handles boules, the oval one handles batards. That covers the two most common artisan loaf shapes, so you’re not locked into one style. Looking at the data, 24-ounce sourdough starter jar is a decent size for maintaining a small colony. It’s glass with a silver lid, and the kit includes a silicone stirring stick and a color-changing temperature strip. The strip is a nice touch—fermentation temperature matters, and having a rough visual cue beats guessing. Here is another way to look at it. what the specifications tell us is that the jar holds enough for regular baking without needing to keep a massive amount of flour active. The included kitchen scale is where things get a bit more complicated. It’s a 10 kg (22 lb* (based on available data)) capacity scale, which is more than enough for dough. But the product listing doesn’t specify readability or graduation increments. For sourdough, you typically want 1-gram precision, especially when measuring starter or salt. A scale that only reads in 5-gram steps will be noticeably less useful for compact adjustments. Based on the available specs, I’d assume this is a fundamental digital scale—fine for flour and water, but don’t expect lab-grade accuracy. The bread lame comes with five replacement blades and a blade cover, which is practical. The Danish whisk is a coil-style tool that works well for mixing high-hydration doughs without overworking them. Based on the available information, silicone bread sling and baking mat are useful for transferring and baking without sticking. The kit also includes a plastic scraper, a stainless steel scraper, a flour sifter, and six bread stencil molds for scoring patterns. That’s a lot of plastic and silicone pieces, and their long-term durability will vary—the scrapers and stencil molds are fine for occasional use, but they’re not heavy-duty tools. Who this is for This kit is for someone who wants to start making artisan sourdough at home and doesn’t already own most of the tools. If you’re buying your first banneton baskets, starter jar, lame, and scale separately, you’ll spend more and deal with multiple shipments. The PeacePeo set consolidates that into one purchase. Here is what stands out from the specs: it’s also a practical gift for a friend or family member who’s shown interest in bread baking but hasn’t committed yet—the variety of items gives them a real starting point. Who should look elsewhere If you already own a good kitchen scale, a lame, and a couple of bannetons, this set will duplicate things you don’t need. The scale’s precision is unclear, and the plastic accessories (scrapers, stencil molds) aren’t upgrades over what a serious baker might already have. Also, if you only bake one shape of loaf, the two-basket set is more than you require. In that case, buying a single basket and a separate starter jar would be simpler and likely cheaper.
