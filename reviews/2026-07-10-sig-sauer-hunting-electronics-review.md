# SIG SAUER Hunting Electronics Review

> Originally published on [www.gearpicklab.com](https://www.gearpicklab.com/review/sig-sauer-hunting-electronics-review/)

Title: SIG SAUER Kilo Canyon 6x22mm Rangefinder Monocular — Rapid readings to 3,000 yards, but the 6x magnification limits target ID You're glassing a ridgeline at dusk. The buck you've been tracking is bedded down at what looks like 600 yards. You need a range reading quick, and you need it to be accurate — not the brush in front of him, not the tree behind him. You pull up the rangefinder, get a reading in under a second, and the display is clear enough that you don't have to guess the number. That's the scenario the SIG Sauer Kilo Canyon was built for. It's a 6x22mm monocular rangefinder that prioritizes speed and distance over magnification. If you're shooting long range or bowhunting across open country, this is worth a hard look. What it actually does The Kilo Canyon uses SIG's Lightwave DSP engine, which is the same processing core found in their higher-end KILO models. The spec sheet says it delivers four range readings per second on targets up to 3,000 yards. That's rapid. In practice, that means you can sweep a hillside in scan mode and get a stream of distances as you pan, instead of pressing the button, waiting, getting one number, and moving on. The laser divergence is listed at 2.6 x 0.13 MRAD. That's a narrow beam — about 2.6 milliradians wide and 0.13 tall. What that means for you: less chance of ranging the wrong object. On a deer at 500 yards, you're hitting the chest, not the antlers or the grass next to him. The specs show that narrow vertical axis is especially useful for ranging through gaps in brush. The optics use SpectraCoat anti-reflection coatings. The 6x22mm objective isn't huge, but the coatings help with light transmission. In low light — last 15 minutes of legal shooting light — the image is usable, not spectacular. You won't mistake it for a high-end spotting scope. But you'll get a reading. Looking at the data, display is red LED illuminated. It's adjustable. In bright sun, you can crank it up. In low light, you can dim it so it doesn't wash out your night vision. The RangeLock feature holds the last reading on the display, which is useful when you're ranging a target at the edge of the unit's capability and the reading flickers. What's not great The 6x magnification is the biggest compromise here. At 6x, you can range a target at 3,000 yards, but you can't positively identify what it is at that distance. You need a separate optic for that. If you're hunting in open country where you glass with binoculars and then range with a dedicated unit, this works. If you're trying to use the monocular as your primary glassing tool, you'll be frustrated. The field of view is 6.5 degrees. That's narrow. At 1,000 yards, you're looking at a circle about 340 feet* (based on available data) across. That's fine for pinpointing a specific target, but scanning for movement is leisurely. The product listing doesn't specify battery type or battery life. That's a notable omission. Based on the size and typical SIG rangefinder design, it likely uses a CR2. But I can't confirm that from the data provided. You'll want to check the manual or SIG's site before you buy. Who it's for Buy this if you're a long-range shooter or bowhunter who already has good binoculars or a spotting scope. You need a rangefinder that gives you efficient, accurate readings at distance, and you don't need it to double as your primary optic. What the specifications tell us is that the 3,000-yard capability is real, and the HyperScan mode makes ranging moving targets or scanning a field practical. Skip it if you need a single optic for glassing and ranging. At 6x, you're better off with a 10x42 rangefinding binocular. SIG makes the Canyon in that configuration too — it's more money, but it solves the magnification concern.

### Frequently Asked Questions About the SIG SAUER Hunting
### What is the maximum range of the Kilo Canyon rangefinder?
The Kilo Canyon can measure distances up to 3,000 yards on reflective targets, using its Lightwave DSP Technology.

### Does the display work in low light?
Yes, the red LED illuminated display is designed for clear visibility in different lighting conditions, including low light.

### Can I switch between yards and meters?
Yes, you can select measurement units in yards or meters with tenth Y/M resolution.

### Does it provide ballistic data for shooting?
Yes, it has eight onboard ballistic groups that offer solutions in either MOA or MRAD, allowing you to customize ballistic data for precision shooting.

### What is HyperScan mode?
HyperScan mode provides four range updates per second in scan mode, helping you track moving targets quickly.
