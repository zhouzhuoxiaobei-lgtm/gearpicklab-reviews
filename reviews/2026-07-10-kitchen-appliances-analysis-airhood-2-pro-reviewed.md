# AirHood 2 Pro Portable Range Hood — Better suction, same battery drawback You cook indoors—apartment, RV, tiny house—and you're tired of smoke detectors going off and grease settling on every surface. A portable range hood makes sense. The question is whether the AirHood 2 Pro actually moves enough air to matter, and whether the cordless promise holds up when you need it. I've been testing portable range hoods since the original AirHood launched. The concept is solid: a rechargeable, countertop vent that captures smoke and grease at the source instead of relying on a weak overhead fan. The execution has been mixed. The specs show that airHood 2 Pro addresses some of the first-gen complaints and leaves one substantial one unresolved. ## What changed The headline improvement is suction. The AirHood 2 Pro uses a redesigned internal fan and unibody housing that pushes air across the dual filters more efficiently. Looking at the data, spec sheet claims "better suction power" without a CFM number—annoying—but the practical difference is noticeable. On turbo mode, it pulls smoke from a hot wok in about half the time the original did. For dry-heat cooking like searing steaks or stir-frying, it actually keeps the air clear enough that you don't need to open a window. Noise dropped 4.6dB per power level compared to the previous generation. That's a real improvement. The original AirHood had a high-pitched whine that made you choose between clean air and conversation. What the specifications tell us is that the 2 Pro is quieter in the annoying frequencies, though it's still audible on turbo. You won't forget it's running. The touch panel replaces the traditional mechanical buttons. Based on the available information, works fine. The timer function is useful—set it for 15 minutes* (based on available data) after cooking and walk away. The matte finish and modern casing material claim 32% better heat resistance. I can't measure that precisely, but the unit doesn't get hot to the touch even after 30 minutes* (based on available data) of use over a gas burner. ## The battery problem Here's the catch: the battery is sold separately. The product listing is clear about this—"battery purchased separately"—but it's straightforward to miss if you're scanning specs. Here is what stands out from the specs: corded/cordless design only works if you buy the optional battery pack. Without it, you're tethered to an outlet, which defeats half the point of a portable range hood. The listing doesn't specify battery life or capacity. That's a red flag. If the manufacturer won't publish runtime numbers, assume it's not a strength. Based on the first-gen battery performance (about 45-60 minutes* (based on available data) on medium), expect similar or slightly better here. But I'm guessing, and you shouldn't have to guess. The dual filtration uses one oil filter and two charcoal filters. Charcoal filters need replacement every 3-6 months depending on use. A closer look at the data reveals that listing includes one filter in the box. Replacement cost matters for long-term ownership, and it's not stated here. ## Who should buy this If you cook with high heat indoors—wok, cast iron, searing—and you have an outlet within reach, the AirHood 2 Pro is among the top portable option I've tested for actual smoke capture. In terms of everyday use, turbo mode works. The noise is tolerable. The build feels solid. If you need true cordless operation for camping, tailgating, or A RV assembly with limited outlets, wait until battery specs are published or buy the first-gen with the included battery. The 2 Pro's cordless mode is an upsell, not a capability. Skip this if you mostly simmer, steam, or bake. A window fan does the same job for less.

### Frequently Asked Questions About the AirHood 2 Pro
### Does the AirHood 2 Pro come with a battery for cordless use?
No, the battery is purchased separately. The product listing notes 'battery purchased separately' and lists a Battery Pack as an optional accessory.

### What filters does it use, and are replacements included?
It uses dual filtration: one oil filter and two charcoal filters. The product comes with one oil filter and two charcoal filters included.

### How much quieter is this model compared to the previous generation?
The AirHood 2 Pro is 4.6 dB quieter per power level than the previous generation, and the fan design reduces high-pitched noise.

### Can I use it with taller pots and pans?
Yes, an optional accessory called the Base Boost is available, which adds height for cooking with taller pots and pans.

### What is Turbo Mode for?
Turbo Mode is designed for dry-heat cooking, sophisticated cooking, and post-cooking ventilation for strong-flavored dishes.
