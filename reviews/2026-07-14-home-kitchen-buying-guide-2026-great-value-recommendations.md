- **Check the materials.** If the product listing doesn't say what it's made of, assume it's the cheapest option. Stainless steel, borosilicate glass, and food-grade silicone are capable signs. Aluminum, plastic, and "ceramic coating" (which is just paint) are red flags.

- **Look for specific numbers.** A "high-powered motor" means nothing. A "1200W* (based on available data) motor" tells you exactly what you're getting. The Permasteel Power Tool doesn't list wattage, but it does list the blade material (stainless steel) and the capacity (enough for a full batch). That's better than vague marketing.
