# Key Specifications of the Huepar Professional Laser Explained

Huepar TPD14B Laser Level Tripod: A Solid Stand for Real Job Sites For anyone considering this product, why a dedicated tripod matters. The Huepar TPD14B is a 60-inch stand built to hold heavier gear like rotary lasers and distance measurers, not just a point-and-shoot camera. It is designed for people who need the tool to stay put and stay level. What matters about this tripod The head is the part that usually frustrates you on inexpensive stands. Huepar uses a 3-way pan head with 360° horizontal rotation and 90° vertical tilt. That means you can point a laser where you need it without fighting the locks. There is a separate knob for the positioning arm, so you can tweak the angle without loosening everything else. A built-in bubble level is there to help you confirm you are square before you start laying out a wall or a ceiling grid. The legs are four-section with height-locking buckles, and there is an extendable center column. The specs show that range is roughly 18.7 inches collapsed up to 60 inches fully extended. That is enough height for most interior work—setting cabinet heights, marking electrical boxes, or aligning tile layouts. The load capacity is rated at 22 pounds (10 kg), which covers heavy-duty laser levels and larger distance measurers without feeling top-heavy. Looking at the data, quick release plate uses a 1/4"-20 screw mount, and it comes with a 5/8"-11 adapter nut. That dual-thread installation is practical because it covers both standard camera threads and the larger thread used on many construction lasers. You can swap between a laser level and a camera or a distance measurer without needing a separate adapter. What to consider before buying The tripod weighs about 2.4 pounds* (based on available data) (1.11 kg). That is light enough to carry around a job site in the included storage bag, but it is not so light that it feels flimsy. The construction is anodized aluminum and high-strength ABS plastic. What the specifications tell us is that the aluminum is in the center column and braces, which is where you want the strength. The plastic parts are mostly in the leg locks and head assembly—they feel solid enough, but if you drop it on concrete regularly, those are the parts that might wear first. The rubber feet are rotatable, which helps on uneven ground, and there is a multi-function hook on the center column. You can hang a sandbag or a tool bag from it to add stability outdoors or on windy days. That is a useful feature, but the tripod does not come with a weight bag, so you will need to supply your own. One thing the listing does not specify is the exact material of the leg locks or the head mechanism. Based on the description, the build seems aimed at regular job-site use rather than occasional DIY. If you are a contractor using it daily, it should hold up. If you are a weekend warrior, it will likely outlast your projects. Based on the available information, the key takeaway The Huepar TPD14B is a functional, no-nonsense tripod for anyone who demands to mount a laser level, point laser, or distance measurer at a consistent height. The 3-way head and dual-thread plate make it all-around across different tools, and the 60-inch max height covers most interior work. It is not the lightest or the cheapest stand you can find online, but the build quality and load capacity suggest it is meant for real work, not just a shelf. If you need a stable base for a heavy laser and you are tired of jury-rigging solutions, this one is worth a look.
