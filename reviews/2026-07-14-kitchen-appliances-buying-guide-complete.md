 ### How to Read Spec Sheets Like a Pro

Manufacturers love to hide the bad stuff. Here’s what to look for:

 - **Wattage vs. Actual Performance:** A 1,500-watt blender that lists “peak power” is lying. Look for “continuous power” or “rated power.”
 - **Noise Levels:** If a product doesn’t list decibel ratings, assume it’s loud. For most buyers, airHood 2 Pro lists 45 dB* (based on available data) on low — that’s whisper-quiet. Most don’t.
 - **Materials:** “Stainless steel” on the outside often means plastic inside. The MegaWise 5 has a stainless steel blade but a plastic drive shaft — that’s a weak point.
 - **Warranty:** One year is standard. Two years is good. Anything less is a red flag.
