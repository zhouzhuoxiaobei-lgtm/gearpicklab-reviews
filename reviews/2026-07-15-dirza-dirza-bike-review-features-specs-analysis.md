Title: Dirza Bike Wall Mount Bike Hanger 2 Pack — A no-frills horizontal rack with a 38-pound limit and a notable fit restriction If you’re storing a mountain bike or road bike in a garage or shed, and you want it off the floor without drilling into a stud on a regular basis, this two-pack from Dirza does exactly what the spec sheet promises. The foldable arm is the standout feature here—it lets you collapse the hook flat against the wall when not in use, which is genuinely useful for a wall rack. But the 38-pound weight limit and the explicit exclusion of hybrid bikes and bikes without a straight horizontal top tube mean this isn’t a universal answer. The specs show that rack weighs 3 pounds per unit, which is on the lighter side for a wall-mount bike hanger. That’s fine for a road bike or a portable mountain bike, but if your bike is on the heavier end of the 38-pound limit, you’ll want to make absolutely sure you’re mounting into a stud or concrete—the instructions emphasize this, and the spec sheet doesn’t mention any drywall anchors or toggle bolts in the box. The foldable arm is a practical capability: when you’re not using the rack, you can fold it flat against the wall, which keeps the garage or shed less cluttered. The rubber coating on the hook does prevent scratches, but it’s a thin coating, not a padded sleeve—so don’t expect it to protect a delicate carbon frame from minor scuffs. Looking at the data, installation hardware is included, but the spec sheet doesn’t list what type of screws or anchors come in the box. Limitations & data gaps The most obvious gap is the lack of any warranty or load-testing data. The spec sheet says “sturdy” but doesn’t give a cycle rating or a safety factor. For a product that holds a bike overhead, that’s worth noting. What the specifications tell us is that the instruction manual is mentioned but not described—no clue whether it’s a single diagram or a multi-step guide. Also missing: any mention of the hook’s internal width or the diameter of the tubing it can accommodate. If your bike’s top tube is unusually thick or thin, you’re guessing. Based on the available information, product listing also doesn’t specify the material of the rack itself—likely steel, but not confirmed. Summary This two-pack is a straightforward horizontal wall rack for standard mountain and road bikes with a straight top tube, offering a foldable arm and rubber coating for basic scratch protection. The 38-pound weight limit and the exclusion of hybrid bikes are the key constraints—make sure your bike fits both the weight and frame shape before buying. If you need a heavier-duty rack or one that works with non-standard frames, you’ll want to look elsewhere. Who this is for This works best for someone with one or two standard mountain or road bikes who wants a minimal, space-saving wall storage resolution. The foldable arm makes it a good pick for a garage or shed where you might need the wall space back when the bikes are out. If you’re a casual rider with a light bike and you don’t mind drilling into studs, this is a practical, no-fuss option. Who should look elsewhere Skip this if you own a hybrid bike, a bike with a curved or non-straight top tube, or any bike over 38 pounds. The fit restriction is hard and efficient—the spec sheet explicitly says “NOT for hybrid bikes or bikes that do not have straight horizontal frame.” Also, if you need a rack that can handle an e-bike or a heavy downhill rig, the 38-pound limit is too low.
