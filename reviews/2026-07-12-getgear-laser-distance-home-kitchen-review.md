Title: Getgear Laser Distance Measure Case for PREXISO Dual Laser Measure — Semi-hard shell, but check your fit If you own a PREXISO Dual Laser Measure (the 230Ft rechargeable model), you already know the tool itself is solid. The question is whether the factory case holds up to job site abuse or if you need to buy an aftermarket option. This Getgear case is one of those aftermarket options. The specs show that it’s a semi-hard zippered case with a mesh pocket and elastic strap inside. The materials are PEVA—water-resistant and shock-absorbing. That’s the entry-level pitch. Here’s what the specs tell you and what they don’t. What it does The case is built around one specific device: the PREXISO Dual Laser Measure. That means the interior cutout and strap positions are designed to hold that tool snugly. Looking at the data, semi-hard shell is stiffer than a fabric pouch but softer than a hard plastic clamshell. It’ll protect against scratches, dust, and minor drops onto concrete from waist height. For a laser measure that can cost over that’s a meaningful upgrade over a cardboard box or a loose tool bag pocket. What the specifications tell us is that the mesh pocket adds utility. It’s substantial enough for the [USB-C](https://www.usb.org/usb-type-c) charging cable, a small wall adapter, and maybe a spare battery if the tool uses one (the PREXISO unit is rechargeable, so you’re mostly storing the cable). The elastic strap secures the laser measure in place during transit. At this spec level, most dedicated cases for laser measures run between and This one sits in the mid-range of that bracket. It’s not a premium Pelican-style hard case, but it doesn’t pretend to be. Limitations The biggest limitation is specificity: this case is designed only for the PREXISO Dual Laser Measure. If you have a different brand or model, the fit may be loose or the strap placement wrong. The product listing is clear about that, but it’s worth repeating because generic “laser measure cases” often claim compatibility that doesn’t hold up. Based on the available information, other limitation is protection depth. Semi-hard PEVA is satisfactory for scratches and light impacts, but it won’t protect against heavy drops or crushing. If you throw your tool bag into the back of a truck bed regularly, this case is better than nothing but not as protective as a hard plastic case with foam inserts. Here is what stands out from the specs: listing doesn’t specify drop-test ratings or crush resistance, so assume it handles normal daily use, not abuse. There’s also no mention of a belt clip or carabiner loop. If you need to carry the case on your belt, you’ll need to add your own attachment. Who this is for This case is for someone who owns the PREXISO Dual Laser Measure and wants a dedicated, organized way to carry it and its charging cable. It’s also for someone who wants better protection than a fabric pouch but doesn’t need a hard case. If you work in a controlled environment—indoor finish work, home DIY, light construction—this is a practical upgrade. Who should look elsewhere If you own a different laser measure brand or model, skip this. The fit is tool-specific. Also skip it if you need heavy-duty protection—job sites with frequent drops, rain exposure, or crushing loads. For that, look for a hard case with foam cutouts and an IP rating. And if you prefer to keep your tool in a multi-pocket tool bag without a separate case, you don’t need this at all. Image alt text:
