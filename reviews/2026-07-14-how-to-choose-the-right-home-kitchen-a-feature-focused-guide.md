 Sometimes the most important info is what’s not listed. Based on the available information, **Prdingfy Sourdough Starter** doesn’t mention the jar’s capacity in ounces or milliliters.  ### How to Read Product Listings Like a Pro

 I’ve analyzed thousands of product pages. Here’s what I look for:

 - **Specific numbers:** Watts, RPM, dB, temperature range, weight capacity, dimensions. If it’s vague, it’s marketing.
 - **Material names:** “Stainless steel” is better than “metal.” “Borosilicate glass” is better than “glass.”
 - **Certifications:** [IP67](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IP_Code) (waterproof), NSF (food safety), UL (electrical safety). If they’re not mentioned, they probably don’t have them.
 - **Real customer photos:** Look for images that show the product in use, not just studio shots. That tells you the actual size and finish.

 Avoid listings that use phrases like “perfect for any kitchen” or “worth considering gadget.” Those are filler words. Here is what stands out from the specs: best product pages tell you exactly what it does, how it does it, and what it’s made of.
