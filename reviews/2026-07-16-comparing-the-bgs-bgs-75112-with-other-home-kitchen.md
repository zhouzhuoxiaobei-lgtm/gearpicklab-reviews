Title: BGS 75112 Water Pump Pliers — Solid build, but the 44mm opening limits its range If you’re grabbing a set of water pump pliers for plumbing work, you’re usually trading off between jaw capacity and grip strength. The BGS 75112 lands at 300 mm in length with a 44 mm maximum opening — that’s enough for standard pipe fittings and nuts up to about 1¾ inches, but not much beyond that. These are box-joint pliers, which means the pivot slot runs through a channel in the jaw rather than relying on a single rivet. That design tends to keep the jaws aligned under load better than slip-joint types, especially when you’re torquing on a stubborn fitting. The opening width of 44 mm is functional for 1-inch to 1½-inch pipe, but it won’t handle larger drain pipes or 2-inch fittings. There’s no mention of a modern or wire-crimping notch, so these are dedicated gripping pliers — no multitasking for electrical work. Summary The BGS 75112 is a well-constructed pair of box-joint pliers that uses chrome vanadium steel with forging and hardening for durability. The 300 mm length and jamming protection make it a solid choice for standard plumbing and mechanical work where the 44 mm jaw capacity is sufficient. If you need to handle larger pipe diameters or want a lighter tool for overhead use, the missing weight spec and limited opening width suggest you should check alternatives with more capacity. Who this is for This is for someone doing regular plumbing repairs or mechanical assembly where 1½-inch fittings are the max. If you’re a homeowner fixing a leaky sink or a maintenance tech swapping out valves, the 300 mm length gives you enough leverage without being unwieldy. What the specifications tell us is that the box-joint design and jamming protection mean you’re not fighting the tool while you’re turning. Who should look elsewhere If you work with 2-inch pipe or larger, or if you need a pair of pliers that can also cut wire or crimp terminals, this isn’t the one.
